Key Listening Strategies

In NYLC listening tasks, focus on specific facts (figures, names, processes) and implied meaning — what can logically be inferred? Speakers often signal key ideas with phrases like "What's important is..." or "Going forward..." Listen for these cues.

Business Reading

Article on employee motivation and workforce retention strategies

Reading Strategy: Skim, Then Scan

Skim first (30–45 seconds): Read the title, the first and last sentence of each paragraph. Form a mental map of the argument. Then scan for specific information when answering — search for the key words in each option, not the whole sentence.

Article Summary

Recent research reveals that companies misunderstand what motivates employees to stay. While managers assume salary is the primary driver, workers rank recognition of individual achievement most highly. During the economic downturn, many accepted roles below their qualification level. Consultants such as Brian Wilkinson recommend investing in staff retention programmes. Remote and flexible working may grow in popularity, but is unlikely to become the standard permanent arrangement for most firms.

Vocabulary in Context

remuneration
salary and financial compensation package
economic downturn
a period of reduced economic activity or recession
conceal
to hide or deliberately keep something secret
invest in
to allocate resources (money, time) to something
permanent
lasting or intended to last indefinitely

Formal Business Writing

Master the five-part structure of a professional business letter

The 5 Parts of a Business Letter

1. Heading — Your address and the date (top right or left).
2. Inside Address — The recipient's name and full address.
3. Salutation — Dear Mr/Ms [Surname], (formal, always with a comma).
4. Body — Typically 3–4 paragraphs: opening purpose → main content → impact → closing.
5. Signature — "Yours sincerely" (if you know the name) + your full name.

Body Paragraph Guide

¶1 — Purpose: "I am writing to express my sincere gratitude for..."
¶2 — The event: Describe the occasion and the gift specifically and warmly.
¶3 — Significance: Explain what the recognition means to you personally and professionally.
¶4 — Close: Express your continued commitment and look forward to the future.

Letter Template
[Your Name]  ·  Lederer, Lederer and Hall  ·  407 East 23rd Street, New York, NY 10013
[Date]

Mr. Gerald Hall
Lederer, Lederer and Hall
407 East 23rd Street, New York, New York 10013

Dear Mr. Hall,

I am writing to express my heartfelt gratitude for the wonderful party held in my honour to mark my tenth anniversary with the firm, and for the beautiful wristwatch you presented to me as a token of the company's appreciation...

[Your paragraphs — describe the event, the gift's significance, your feelings, and close warmly]

Yours sincerely,
[Your Name]

Business Presentation Skills

Prepare and deliver a 2-minute persuasive product presentation · 20 points

The NYLC Presentation Formula (2 minutes)

Hook (15s) — Open with a question, statistic, or surprising statement to grab the audience's attention.
Introduction (15s) — Name the product and state clearly who it is designed for.
How it works (45s) — Explain the mechanism simply, using clear signposting language.
Benefits (30s) — Why will it improve consumers' lives? Use specific, concrete examples.
Close (15s) — End with a memorable statement or call to action.
